# How It Works: Alumni Engagement Platform

## Overview

Gen-Connect operates as a five-step ecosystem that connects major STEM companies, their diverse employees, public schools, and students from underrepresented backgrounds. Our platform handles all operational complexity to deliver authentic social mobility impact with zero burden on schools.

---

## The Five-Step Process

<img width="2312" height="1362" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/fb9b87bf-6d21-4c12-b9e9-5baecf1a77c3" />

### 1. Corporate Partnership ü§ù

**Partner with STEM companies to identify diverse employee mentors**

- Target companies: Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta, and other major STEM employers
- Identify diverse employees willing to mentor students from similar backgrounds
- Activate Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) and D&I networks
- Leverage existing employee volunteering programs ("impact hours")

### 2. Digital Matchmaking üîó

**Platform connects mentors with students based on alumni ties, background & interests**

- Automated matching algorithm considers:
  - Alumni connections (same school or local area)
  - Demographic background alignment (SES, ethnicity, gender)
  - Student interests and career aspirations
  - Mentor expertise and availability
- Creates "relatable" mentor-student pairs that maximize impact

### 3. Placement Facilitation üìã

**We handle all logistics with zero school burden**

Our platform coordinates:
- **DBS checks** - Enhanced disclosure and barring service checks for all mentors
- **Safeguarding** - Full compliance with child protection regulations
- **Insurance** - Liability coverage for all placements
- **Scheduling** - Coordinate timing between companies, schools, and students
- **Risk assessments** - Complete documentation and compliance

**Result:** Schools get quality placements without administrative overhead

### 4. Student Impact üéØ

**2-week work placement with relatable mentor + optional ongoing support**

- **Primary intervention:** 2-week work experience placement (typically Year 10)
- Students work alongside mentors from similar backgrounds
- Exposure to real STEM careers and professional environments
- **Optional ongoing mentorship:**
  - Support through GCSE choices
  - Guidance during A-Level selection
  - University application support
  - Long-term relationship building

### 5. Measurable Outcomes üìä

**Track student decisions and provide corporate ESG/D&I impact reporting**

We measure and report:
- **Student outcomes:**
  - GCSE subject choices (pre/post intervention)
  - A-Level pathway uptake (STEM vs. non-STEM)
  - University applications and acceptances
  - Perception changes ("STEM is for people like me")
  - Confidence and career clarity metrics

- **Corporate impact reporting:**
  - Number of students influenced
  - Measurable behavior change (% choosing STEM pathways)
  - ESG/CSR metrics for stakeholder reporting
  - Social Mobility Index contribution
  - Employee engagement data (mentor satisfaction, retention)

## Why This Model Works

### 1. Relatability Drives Impact
Matching students with mentors from similar backgrounds creates authentic connection that changes perceptions. Generic STEM outreach lacks this critical element.

### 2. Zero Friction for Schools
By handling all operational complexity (DBS, safeguarding, insurance, scheduling), we eliminate the barrier that prevents schools from offering quality placements.

### 3. Activates Untapped Corporate Resource
Companies have diverse employees who want to give back but lack structured pathways. We provide the infrastructure to activate this hidden asset.

### 4. Measurable ROI for Corporate Partners
Unlike generic CSR donations, we track quantifiable student outcomes that demonstrate authentic social mobility impact for ESG reporting.

### 5. Intervention at Critical Decision Point
Year 10 work experience happens when students are making career-shaping GCSE choices. Early intervention prevents doors from closing.
