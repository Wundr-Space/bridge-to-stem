# Gen-Connect Prezi Pitch Deck - Build Guide

## Overview

This guide provides complete instructions for building the Gen-Connect startup pitch deck in Prezi, leveraging its zooming presentation interface to create an engaging investor experience.

---

## Brand Guidelines

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors:**
- **Deep Blue:** `#1E3A8A` (Trust & Professionalism)
  - Use for: Headings, primary text, key stats, corporate elements
- **Warm Orange:** `#F97316` (Energy & Connection)
  - Use for: Highlights, CTAs, accents, emphasis points

**Secondary Colors:**
- **White:** `#FFFFFF` (Backgrounds, contrast text)
- **Light Gray:** `#F3F4F6` (Card backgrounds, section dividers)
- **Dark Gray:** `#374151` (Body text, secondary information)

### Typography

**Font Family:** Inter or Arial (for compatibility)
- **Headings:** Bold, 36-48pt
- **Subheadings:** Semi-bold, 24-28pt
- **Body text:** Regular, 14-18pt
- **Stats/Numbers:** Bold, 40-60pt

### Visual Style

- **Modern and clean:** Avoid clutter, use white space effectively
- **Professional:** Trust-building for corporate audiences
- **Impact-focused:** Lead with data and outcomes
- **Personal:** Include relatable human elements (student/mentor stories)

## Prezi-Specific Design Tips

### Navigation Flow

1. **Start at Title (center)** - Large, impactful
2. **Zoom out to overview** - Show Problem â†’ Solution â†’ Impact at high level
3. **Zoom into Problem** - Deep dive on pain points (Slide 2)
4. **Zoom into Solution** - 5-step process (Slide 3)
5. **Pan to UVP** - Differentiation (Slide 4)
6. **Zoom into Business Model** - How we make money (Slide 5)
7. **Zoom out to Market** - TAM/SAM/SOM circles (Slide 6)
8. **Pan to GTM** - 3 channels side-by-side (Slide 7)
9. **Zoom out to Impact** - Big vision + flywheels (Slide 8)
10. **Zoom into Team** - Human element (Slide 9)
11. **Pan to Traction** - Validation checkmarks (Slide 10)
12. **Pan to Timeline** - Roadmap + budget (Slide 11)
13. **Zoom back to center for CTA** - Final ask (Slide 12)

### Transition Recommendations

- **Use smooth zoom transitions** - Not jarring jumps
- **Group related content** - Keep related slides physically close on canvas
- **Use rotation sparingly** - Only for emphasis (e.g., stats spinning in)
- **Path order matters** - Test flow multiple times for narrative coherence

### Visual Hierarchy

- **Largest text:** Stats and key numbers (60-72pt)
- **Medium text:** Headings and section titles (36-48pt)
- **Smallest text:** Body and details (14-18pt)
- **Use color to guide eye:** Warm Orange for highlights, Deep Blue for structure

### Interactive Elements

- **Clickable areas:** Allow audience to explore sections non-linearly (if presenting live)
- **Hidden details:** Nest additional info under main points (zoom into for Q&A)
- **Back buttons:** Create paths to return to overview slide

## Final Notes

**Keep It Visual:**
- Maximum 3-5 bullet points per zoom level
- Use images and icons to replace text where possible
- White space is your friend

**Tell a Story:**
- Lead with emotion (student stories, personal mission)
- Build credibility (traction, validation)
- Close with urgency (why now, what you need)

**Practice the Flow:**
- Rehearse zoom transitions until smooth
- Time yourself (aim for 10-12 minutes + Q&A)
- Prepare for non-linear navigation (investor questions)

**Make It Accessible:**
- High contrast for readability
- Avoid small text (minimum 14pt)
- Test on different screen sizes
